# Final Recommendation

## Objective

The objective of this analysis was to identify the factors most strongly associated with Monthly Sales performance across retail stores and provide evidence-based recommendations for leadership.

Regression analysis was performed to evaluate the relationship between Monthly Sales and several business variables including Marketing Spend, Footfall, Inventory Availability Percentage, Customer Rating, and Store Type.

---

## Key Findings

The Multiple Regression Model achieved the strongest explanatory power with:

- R² = 0.8116
- Adjusted R² = 0.8086

The model explains approximately 81.16% of the variation in Monthly Sales and therefore provides a strong basis for business decision-making.

---

## Factors Most Strongly Associated with Monthly Sales

### Footfall

Footfall was the strongest predictor in the final model.

The coefficient indicates a strong positive relationship between customer traffic and Monthly Sales. Stores with higher footfall consistently generated stronger sales performance.

### Marketing Spend

Marketing Spend showed a positive and statistically significant relationship with Monthly Sales.

Higher marketing investment was associated with increased sales performance, suggesting that marketing activities contribute to revenue growth.

### Inventory Availability Percentage

Inventory Availability Percentage was also a significant positive predictor of Monthly Sales.

Stores maintaining higher inventory availability were associated with stronger sales results, highlighting the importance of effective inventory management.

### Customer Rating

Customer Rating demonstrated a positive and statistically significant relationship with Monthly Sales.

Stores with higher customer ratings generally achieved better sales performance, suggesting that customer experience plays an important role in business outcomes.

---

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

### Store_Airport

The Store_Airport variable produced unreliable statistical output and should not be used for business conclusions.

Additional investigation would be required before making decisions based on store-type differences.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

Based on the regression results, leadership should prioritize the following actions:

1. Increase customer footfall through targeted marketing campaigns and customer acquisition initiatives.
2. Continue investing in marketing activities that demonstrate measurable impact on customer traffic and sales.
3. Maintain high inventory availability to reduce stock-out risk and lost sales opportunities.
4. Improve customer experience and service quality to support stronger customer ratings.
5. Review operational practices of high-performing stores and replicate successful strategies across the retail network.

---

## Risks and Limitations

Several limitations should be considered when interpreting the results:

- The model explains a large proportion of sales variation but does not explain all factors affecting performance.
- Some business drivers may not be included in the dataset.
- Local market conditions, competitive intensity, economic factors, and management quality may influence sales outcomes.
- Residual analysis identified stores that performed substantially above or below model expectations, indicating that additional factors remain unmeasured.

---

## Why Regression Does Not Prove Causation

Regression analysis identifies statistical associations between variables and Monthly Sales.

However, regression alone cannot prove that a variable directly causes changes in sales performance.

For example:

- Higher footfall is associated with higher sales, but increasing footfall alone does not guarantee increased revenue.
- Marketing activities may influence both customer traffic and sales simultaneously.
- Other external factors may affect both the independent variables and sales outcomes.

Therefore, regression results should be interpreted as evidence of association rather than proof of direct causation.

---

## Final Recommendation

The Multiple Regression Model is recommended as the primary decision-support model because it provides the highest explanatory power and incorporates multiple business drivers simultaneously.

The analysis suggests that leadership should focus primarily on:

- Increasing Footfall
- Optimizing Marketing Spend
- Maintaining High Inventory Availability
- Improving Customer Ratings

These variables show the strongest and most reliable association with Monthly Sales and represent the most actionable opportunities for improving store performance and driving business growth.
